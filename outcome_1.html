1.1


When used properly, Technology is one of the most powerful tools in a modern business’s arsenal. Technology can be used to increase efficiency by automating or streamlining various services. For example, checking stock, automatically sending out emails or newsletters, and also allowing employees to work remotely - just to name a few.

Technology has become essential for business marketing strategies, largely due to the rise of social media. Proper navigation of the social media marketplaces such as Instagram, Facebook and YouTube, can help a business to easily target specific groups of people. In addition, when combined with machine learning and artificial intelligence, this data can be extrapolated and harnessed to improve the chosen marketing strategy.

Websites and services such as Squarespace and Wix have made it much easier for individuals and small business owners to create professional landing and product pages for their business. An effective landing page creates a professional image for the business and can help a business to convert more of their traffic into sales.

1.7


The General Data Protection Regulation, or GDPR, is the core of Europe’s digital privacy legislation. The GDPR sets out 7 key principles:

  • Lawfulness, fairness and transparency
  • Purpose limitation
  • Data minimisation
  • Accuracy
  • Storage limitation
  • Integrity and confidentiality
  • Accountability

Digital service providers can encounter big financial sanctions for GDPR infringements. These are hugely detrimental to a business’s reputation, image and cash flow. Therefore, it is important that all aspects of a business or digital project adhere to the seven key principles of the GDPR.
